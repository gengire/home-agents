# Family Food Preferences & Pritikin Guidelines
**Last updated:** May 2, 2026

---

## Household
Chris, wife, and two teenage daughters (4 people total). Both adults work full time — minimal weeknight cooking effort is the priority. Daughters have no special dietary needs and are treated as adults for all meal planning.

---

## Dietary Approach
- Following **Pritikin diet** principles: very low added fat, high fiber, whole foods.
- "Go" foods: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, starchy vegetables, fish, skinless poultry, nonfat dairy or soymilk.
- Flavor with herbs, spices, garlic, onions, citrus, vinegar, mustard, and hot sauce.
- Minimize added oils, butter, full-fat dairy, red/processed meats, refined grains, and added sugars.
- **Weight loss** is a gradual, healthy goal for Chris and his wife. Meals should be satisfying without being calorie-dense.

## Cuisine Preferences

### Indian Food — Household Favorite ⭐
Indian cuisine is the top preference. The spice layering achieves deep flavor without fat — ideal for Pritikin.
- **Loved:** Dal (red lentil, tadka, etc.), chana masala, tikka-style dishes (yogurt-marinated), saag, biryani-style rice, raita as a cooling side, aloo dishes.
- **Key spices:** cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, cardamom, mustard seeds, curry leaves, fenugreek, chili.
- Target: **at least 2 Indian meals per week**, rotating across subcategories (legume, protein, vegetable).

### Other Welcome Cuisines
- Mediterranean (roasted vegetables, chickpea dishes, herb-heavy fish)
- Mexican/Latin (black bean tacos, lentil filling, corn-based dishes — bean and veggie forward)
- Asian stir-fries (light sauce, lots of vegetables)
- American comfort adapted Pritikin-style (turkey chili, stuffed peppers, etc.)

### Flavor Standard
Bold and restaurant-quality. Nothing bland. Chris especially cares about this — "diet food" blandness is not acceptable.

## Desserts (Weekly — Pritikin Compliant)
1–2 desserts per week. Rules: fruit-forward, nonfat dairy, minimal/no added sugar, whole grain/oat bases (no butter).
- **Favorites:** Mango lassi (nonfat yogurt), frozen banana "ice cream", spiced poached pears, berry parfait with oat crumble, kheer (soymilk + cardamom), fruit crisp with oat topping.

## Common Proteins
- Fish: salmon, cod, tilapia, shrimp (target 1x+/week)
- Skinless chicken breast or turkey
- Beans, lentils, chickpeas (especially in Indian dishes)
- Occasional lean ground turkey or very lean beef

## Cooking & Leftover Strategy
- **Goal:** 3–4 cooking sessions cover the full 7-day week.
- **Serving math:** Cook once = eat twice for all 4 people. Default recipe yield is 10–12 servings to cover 2 dinners for 4 + second helpings.
- **Sunday:** Primary batch cook day (no time pressure). Make 2 large recipes.
- **Weeknights:** NEW COOK meals ≤ 45 minutes. Leftovers are preferred, not a fallback.
- Leftovers are planned and expected — not an afterthought.
