Title: Lemon-Garlic Shrimp Pasta with Whole-Wheat Spaghetti and Baby Spinach
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

Large shrimp, peeled and deveined (1.5 lbs, fresh or fully thawed)
Whole-wheat spaghetti (12 oz)
Baby spinach (4 cups)
Garlic cloves, minced (6)
Lemon, zested and juiced (2)
Dry white wine or low-sodium chicken broth (1/2 cup)
Red pepper flakes (1/2 tsp)
Black pepper (to taste)
Fresh parsley, chopped (1/4 cup)
Pasta water, reserved (1 cup)
Mixed greens (for side salad)
Red wine vinegar (for dressing)

Directions:

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the whole-wheat spaghetti according to package directions, typically 10 to 12 minutes for al dente. Before draining, scoop out 1 full cup of pasta water and set it aside. Drain the pasta but do not rinse it.
2. While pasta cooks, pat the shrimp completely dry with paper towels — this is important for getting a proper sear rather than steaming them.
3. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a splash of the white wine or broth, then add the garlic and red pepper flakes. Cook, stirring, for 30 seconds until fragrant but not browned.
4. Add the shrimp to the skillet in a single layer. Cook for 2 minutes on the first side without moving them, then flip and cook 1 to 2 minutes on the second side. Shrimp are done when they are fully pink and opaque, curl into a C-shape, and reach an internal temperature of 145°F. Remove the pan from heat immediately — overcooked shrimp curl into a tight O-shape and become rubbery.
5. Return the pan to medium heat. Add the white wine (or broth), lemon juice, and lemon zest. Let bubble for 1 minute, scraping up any bits from the bottom.
6. Add the drained pasta and baby spinach to the skillet. Toss over low heat, adding reserved pasta water a few tbsp at a time until the sauce lightly coats every strand of pasta. You will likely need 1/4 to 1/2 cup of pasta water.
7. Remove from heat. Stir in the parsley and black pepper. Serve immediately with a simple mixed green salad dressed with red wine vinegar.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 560 kcal
Total Fat: 3.5 g
Saturated Fat: 0.5 g
Cholesterol: 235 mg
Sodium: 370 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 65 g
Dietary Fiber: 9 g
Protein: 52 g
Additional notes: Exceptionally low in fat for a pasta dish; very high protein from shrimp; good source of iron and iodine

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Shrimp is a lean, low-fat seafood approved on the Pritikin program. No butter or cream — the sauce is built entirely from white wine, lemon juice, garlic, and starchy pasta water. Whole-wheat spaghetti provides fiber.
Teen appeal: Shrimp pasta feels special and restaurant-quality; the garlicky lemon sauce is bright and flavorful without being heavy. This is consistently a family-favorite style meal.
Make-ahead tips: This dish is best served immediately as shrimp tighten when reheated. If you must reheat, add a splash of broth and warm gently over low heat. Safety note: watch shrimp carefully — they go from raw to perfectly cooked to overcooked in under 2 minutes per side. Pull them off heat the moment they turn fully pink and opaque.
