Title: Kik Alicha
Category: ETH — Ethiopian
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 55 minutes
Total Time: 70 minutes

Ingredients:

Yellow split peas, dry, rinsed (3 cups)
Yellow onion, finely diced (3 large)
Garlic cloves, minced (8)
Fresh ginger, grated (2 tbsp)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (8 cups)
Turmeric (2 tsp)
Ground cumin (1 tsp)
Ground coriander (1 tsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Lemon, juiced (2)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/4 cup)

Directions:

1. In a large pot, heat 1/4 cup vegetable broth over medium-high heat. Add the diced onions. Cook for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring often, until very soft and golden. Add splashes of broth a tablespoon at a time — the onions should be deeply softened and lightly caramelized, not crisped.
2. Add the garlic and ginger. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring, until very fragrant.
3. Add the turmeric, ground cumin, and ground coriander. Stir constantly for 1 minute. The turmeric will turn the onion base a brilliant, deep yellow — this is the signature color of kik alicha.
4. Add the rinsed yellow split peas. Stir thoroughly to coat evenly with the spiced onion base.
5. Pour in the vegetable broth. Stir well. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Skim any foam from the surface during the first 5 minutes of simmering.
6. Simmer uncovered for 40 to 50 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes, until the split peas are completely soft and have partially dissolved into a thick, porridge-like stew. Add more broth or water 1/2 cup at a time if the stew gets too thick before the peas are fully soft.
7. The stew is ready when a spoon dragged through it holds a line for 2 seconds before filling back in. Stir in the lemon juice, black pepper, and cilantro just before serving.
8. On Tuesday, serve alongside Berbere-Lime Baked Tilapia as a paired dinner. On Wednesday, reheat with a splash of broth and serve over brown rice or barley as a complete meal on its own.

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Entirely plant-based, zero added oil, extraordinarily high fiber. Yellow split peas are one of the most nutrient-dense Pritikin "Go" foods — even higher fiber than red lentils. Kik Alicha is traditionally one of the mildest Ethiopian dishes (no chili in the base), making it a perfect gentle foil for the fiery berbere tilapia.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Stir a big spoonful of berbere paste or chili paste into his bowl. Fresh sliced serrano or a generous drizzle of Tabasco turns this mild stew into a seriously satisfying heat experience without affecting the family's portions.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: With 15g of dietary fiber per serving, this is one of the highest-satiety meals in the plan. A second serving is absolutely fine for both Chris and his wife — the calorie density is very low and the fiber content makes it one of the best appetite-control meals available.

Leftover ideas: Even better on day 2 as the split peas continue to absorb the broth and the flavors meld. Thin with broth when reheating — it will be very thick from the fridge. Works equally well as a grain bowl sauce over barley, or thinned further with extra broth into a hearty soup.

Make-ahead / storage: Refrigerate up to 5 days. Freezes exceptionally well for up to 3 months — the texture becomes even creamier after freezing and thawing. An ideal freezer staple for the rotation.
