Title: Black Bean and Brown Rice Burrito Bowls
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 55 minutes

Ingredients:

Brown rice, dry (2 cups)
Water (4 cups)
Black beans, canned, drained and rinsed (2 cans, 15 oz each)
Corn, frozen, thawed (2 cups)
Salsa, fresh or jarred (1 cup)
Limes, juiced (2)
Ground cumin (1 tsp)
Chili powder (1 tsp)
Jalapeño, minced (1, optional)
Romaine lettuce, shredded (2 cups)
Cherry tomatoes, halved (1 cup)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (small handful, optional)

Directions:

1. Combine brown rice and 4 cups water in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to the lowest setting, cover tightly, and simmer for 45 minutes. Remove from heat and let rest, still covered, for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork. Start this well before the rest of the meal.
2. While the rice cooks, warm the black beans in a small saucepan over medium heat with the cumin, chili powder, and a splash of water. Stir occasionally and cook for about 5 minutes until heated through.
3. Warm the corn in the same pan over medium heat for 2 to 3 minutes, or microwave for 2 minutes.
4. Build each bowl starting with a base of brown rice, then top with black beans, corn, cherry tomatoes, and shredded romaine.
5. Finish each bowl with salsa, a generous squeeze of lime juice, minced jalapeño if desired, and fresh cilantro.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 520 kcal
Total Fat: 3.5 g
Saturated Fat: 0.5 g
Cholesterol: 0 mg
Sodium: 310 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 101 g
Dietary Fiber: 16 g
Protein: 20 g
Additional notes: Cholesterol-free, very high-fiber, excellent plant-based protein source; one of the most Pritikin-compliant meals in the plan

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Entirely plant-based with whole grain brown rice and fiber-rich legumes. No added oil or fat. Salsa and lime juice provide bold flavor naturally.
Teen appeal: Build-your-own bowl format lets everyone customize their toppings; familiar flavors inspired by a favorite restaurant style. Serve jalapeños on the side so heat is optional.
Make-ahead tips: Brown rice can be cooked up to 4 days ahead and stored in the fridge — reheat with a splash of water in the microwave. Beans also reheat easily. Leftovers store well for next-day lunch.
