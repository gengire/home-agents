Title: Red Lentil Dal
Category: IND-L — Indian Legume/Dal
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 40 minutes
Total Time: 55 minutes

Ingredients:

Red lentils, dry, rinsed (3 cups)
Yellow onion, finely diced (2 large)
Tomatoes, roma, diced (4)
Garlic cloves, minced (6)
Fresh ginger, grated (2 tbsp)
Jalapeño, finely minced (2)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (6 cups)
Water (2 cups)
Ground cumin (2 tsp)
Ground coriander (2 tsp)
Turmeric (1.5 tsp)
Garam masala (1.5 tsp)
Mustard seeds (1 tsp)
Lemon, juiced (2)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/3 cup)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)

Directions:

1. In a large pot, heat 1/4 cup broth over medium-high heat. Add mustard seeds and let them pop for 30 seconds.
2. Add the onions and cook for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring regularly, until golden. Add splashes of broth to prevent sticking.
3. Add the garlic, ginger, and jalapeño. Cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
4. Add the cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Stir for 1 minute to toast.
5. Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes until they break down.
6. Add the rinsed red lentils, remaining broth, and water. Stir well. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low.
7. Simmer uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and the dal has thickened to a porridge-like consistency. Add extra water if it gets too thick.
8. Stir in the lemon juice, black pepper, and cilantro. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve over brown rice with a dollop of nonfat yogurt.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1.5 cups dal
Calories: 265 kcal
Total Fat: 1.0 g
Saturated Fat: 0.1 g
Cholesterol: 0 mg
Sodium: 210 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 47 g
Dietary Fiber: 13 g
Sugars: 5.0 g
Protein: 16 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Entirely plant-based, zero added oil, extremely high fiber. Red lentils are one of the best Pritikin "Go" foods — high protein, very low fat, very high fiber.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Stir in extra minced jalapeño or a spoonful of sambal oelek into his bowl. A drizzle of chili oil is not Pritikin — instead, add a full sliced serrano or a tsp of cayenne stirred into just his portion.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Exceptionally low in calories and fat with outstanding fiber — one of the best weight-loss meals in the rotation. A generous second serving is absolutely fine for both adults.

Leftover ideas: Dal thickens overnight — thin with a splash of broth when reheating. Makes an excellent grain bowl base or a quick lunch soup. Stir a spoonful into nonfat yogurt for a savory raita-style dip.

Make-ahead / storage: Refrigerate up to 5 days. Freezes perfectly up to 4 months. Make a double batch and freeze half in portions for busy weeks.
