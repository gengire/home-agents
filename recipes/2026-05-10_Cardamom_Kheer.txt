Title: Cardamom Kheer
Category: DESS — Dessert
Servings: 8–10 (generous portions for a family of 4, lasts several days in the fridge)
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 40 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

Soymilk, unsweetened (6 cups)
White rice, short-grain or arborio (1/2 cup)
Cardamom, ground (2 tsp)
Cinnamon, ground (1/2 tsp)
Honey or pure maple syrup (2 tbsp)
Raisins (1/4 cup)
Saffron threads (small pinch, steeped in 2 tbsp warm water — optional)
Rose water (1/2 tsp — optional, adds authentic Indian floral note)
Pistachios, shelled and roughly chopped (2 tbsp) for garnish

Directions:

1. If using saffron, steep the pinch of threads in 2 tbsp of warm water for at least 10 minutes before starting the kheer.
2. In a wide, heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the soymilk, rice, and cinnamon. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring frequently to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom.
3. Once simmering, reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer uncovered for 30 to 35 minutes, stirring every 3 to 4 minutes, until the rice is very soft and the mixture has thickened noticeably. It will look thin while hot — it thickens significantly as it cools.
4. Stir in the cardamom, honey (or maple syrup), raisins, and saffron water if using. Cook for 3 more minutes.
5. Remove from heat. Stir in rose water if using. Taste and adjust sweetness — add another teaspoon of honey if desired.
6. Pour into individual bowls or a serving dish. Top each bowl with a small pinch of chopped pistachios.
7. Serve warm immediately after Sunday dinner, or refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours for a cold dessert — kheer is excellent both ways and the flavors deepen overnight.

Saturated Fat: 0.3 g
Cholesterol: 0 mg

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Soymilk replaces whole milk and cream, dramatically reducing fat. Only 2 tbsp of honey divided across 8 to 10 servings equals less than 1 tsp of added sugar per person. Raisins provide natural sweetness. The pistachio garnish is purely cosmetic — too small to count as a fat source. Compared to traditional kheer made with whole milk and 4 to 6 tbsp of sugar, this is a dramatic Pritikin improvement that retains the authentic flavor profile.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: N/A — cooling dessert. Chris can add an extra generous pinch of cardamom over his bowl for more spice complexity.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Moderate in calories for a dessert. One half-cup serving is perfectly appropriate on a weight-loss plan. The small amount of starchy rice and soymilk makes this a genuine treat without being a splurge. Avoid a second full serving — the added sugars from raisins and honey accumulate.

Leftover ideas: This dessert is actually best on days 2 through 4, as the cardamom and saffron deepen in the fridge. Stir in a splash of soymilk before serving if it has become very thick during refrigeration.

Make-ahead / storage: Refrigerate up to 5 days — ideally make Sunday evening and enjoy through the week. Do not freeze — soymilk-based kheer separates when thawed.
