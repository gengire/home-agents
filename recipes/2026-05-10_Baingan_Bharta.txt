Title: Baingan Bharta
Category: IND-V — Indian Vegetable
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice as a side dish, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 70 minutes
Total Time: 85 minutes

Ingredients:

Eggplant, large (4, about 5 lbs total)
Yellow onion, finely diced (3 large)
Garlic cloves, minced (8)
Fresh ginger, grated (2 tbsp)
Jalapeño, finely minced (2)
Tomatoes, roma, diced (6)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (1 cup)
Ground cumin (2 tsp)
Ground coriander (2 tsp)
Turmeric (1 tsp)
Garam masala (1.5 tsp)
Chili powder (1 tsp)
Mustard seeds (1 tsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Lemon, juiced (1)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/3 cup)
Nonfat plain yogurt (1/3 cup) for serving

Directions:

1. Roast the eggplants: Set oven rack on the highest level and set broiler to high (500°F). Pierce each eggplant all over with a fork. Place on a foil-lined baking sheet. Broil for 30 to 40 minutes, turning every 10 minutes, until the skin is completely charred and blackened and the eggplants have collapsed and softened through. Alternatively, char directly over a gas stove flame, turning with tongs every 5 minutes. The deep char is essential to the smoky flavor — do not stop early.
2. Transfer the charred eggplants to a colander over the sink. Let cool for 15 minutes. Peel away and discard all the blackened skin. Drain any liquid from the eggplant flesh, then mash roughly with a fork — leave some texture, do not make it completely smooth.
3. While the eggplant cools, heat 1/4 cup vegetable broth in a large wide pan over medium-high heat. Add mustard seeds and let them pop for 30 seconds.
4. Add the diced onions. Cook for 10 minutes until very golden, adding broth splashes to prevent sticking.
5. Add the garlic, ginger, and jalapeño. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring, until fragrant.
6. Add the cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder. Toast for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
7. Add the diced tomatoes. Cook for 8 minutes, stirring often, until they break down into a thick sauce.
8. Add the mashed eggplant to the pan. Stir everything together — the eggplant will absorb the spiced tomato base completely. Cook over medium heat for 8 to 10 more minutes, stirring regularly, until the entire mixture is cohesive, fragrant, and thick.
9. Stir in the lemon juice, black pepper, and most of the cilantro. Taste and adjust seasoning. Top with remaining cilantro and serve with a small drizzle of nonfat yogurt alongside Chana Masala and brown rice.

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Zero added oil — eggplant is roasted dry under the broiler, onions cooked in broth. The char-roasting technique produces the signature smokiness traditionally achieved over an open flame — entirely Pritikin compliant. Eggplant is extremely low in calories and very high in fiber.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Stir in 1 tsp chili paste or 2 fresh minced green chilies into his portion. A few sliced Thai chilies on top add clean, sharp heat. This dish absorbs extra spice beautifully without losing its smoky base character.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Eggplant is one of the lowest-calorie vegetables per cup — even a generous double portion is under 200 kcal. A second serving is absolutely fine and encouraged for both adults. Pairs perfectly with the protein-dense Chana Masala for a complete, filling Pritikin dinner.

Leftover ideas: Serve alongside any Indian dal or as a sandwich spread in a whole-wheat wrap. Baingan bharta develops deeper smoky flavor by day 2. Can be used as a topping over plain brown rice with a spoonful of yogurt.

Make-ahead / storage: Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freezes well for up to 2 months — the smoky flavor actually intensifies after freezing and reheating. Store separately from the chana masala for maximum reheat flexibility.
