Title: Ginger-Sesame Ground Turkey and Bok Choy over Barley
Category: ASIA — Asian
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes

Ingredients:

Lean ground turkey (2 lbs)
Bok choy, trimmed and roughly chopped (4 heads, about 3 lbs)
Carrots, thinly sliced on the diagonal (4 large)
Yellow onion, thinly sliced (2 large)
Garlic cloves, minced (8)
Fresh ginger, grated (3 tbsp)
Low-sodium soy sauce (1/3 cup)
Rice vinegar (3 tbsp)
Sriracha (1 tbsp)
Low-sodium chicken broth (1 cup)
Cornstarch (2 tbsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Sesame seeds, toasted (2 tbsp)
Green onions, sliced (1 bunch)
Pearl barley, dry (3 cups)

Directions:

1. Cook the barley: Combine 3 cups dry pearl barley with 7 cups water in a medium pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 25 to 30 minutes until tender but still slightly chewy. Drain any excess water. Fluff with a fork and set aside. Start this before beginning the stir-fry — both will finish around the same time.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sriracha, and cornstarch until smooth. Set the sauce aside.
3. Heat a very large skillet or wok over high heat. Add 1/3 cup chicken broth — it should sizzle immediately. Add the sliced onions. Stir-fry for 3 to 4 minutes until softened.
4. Add the carrots. Stir-fry for 3 more minutes over high heat. Add splashes of broth as needed to prevent sticking.
5. Add the ground turkey. Break it into small pieces with a wooden spoon. Cook over high heat for 6 to 8 minutes, stirring and breaking up constantly, until fully cooked through with no pink remaining. Add splashes of broth to keep things moving.
6. Add the garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for 1 minute until very fragrant.
7. Add the bok choy in large handfuls — it will wilt dramatically in volume. Toss constantly over high heat for 3 to 4 minutes until the greens are wilted and the white stalks have a slight tender-crisp bite remaining.
8. Pour the sauce mixture over the entire pan. Toss everything together over high heat for 1 to 2 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats every ingredient evenly. Remove from heat immediately.
9. Scatter the toasted sesame seeds and sliced green onions over the top. Serve immediately over pearl barley.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1.5 cups stir-fry + 3/4 cup barley
Calories: 325 kcal
Total Fat: 5.5 g
Saturated Fat: 1.3 g
Cholesterol: 55 mg
Sodium: 350 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 38 g
Dietary Fiber: 8 g
Sugars: 5.0 g
Protein: 28 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: No added oil — the stir-fry is cooked in broth over high heat. Ground turkey replaces traditionally fattier beef or pork. Bok choy is exceptionally nutritious: very high in vitamins C and K, extremely low in calories. Sesame seeds are used as a seasoning only (2 tbsp across 10–12 servings = negligible fat per serving). Pearl barley is a Pritikin-approved whole grain with significant fiber.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Double the sriracha in his bowl. Stir in a tablespoon of chili-garlic sauce or gochujang paste directly into his serving. Sliced fresh serrano or a shake of dried crushed red pepper on top adds clean, sharp heat without affecting anyone else.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Moderate calorie density from the barley base. One serving is a full, satisfying dinner portion for weight loss goals. A second serving is fine for Chris given his size — his wife should stick to one serving unless still noticeably hungry, as the calorie count adds up. Adding extra bok choy to either plate is always a free, zero-calorie option.

Leftover ideas: Reheat stir-fry and barley in a covered pan with 3 to 4 tbsp chicken broth — the barley absorbs the sauce overnight and needs extra liquid to restore moisture. Works well stuffed into a whole-wheat wrap with sriracha and a handful of shredded raw cabbage.

Make-ahead / storage: Store stir-fry and barley separately in the refrigerator — storing together causes the barley to absorb all remaining sauce. Both keep up to 4 days. Barley freezes well; stir-fry is best within 3 days.
