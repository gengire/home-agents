Title: Saag Chicken
Category: IND-P — Indian Protein
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 65 minutes

Ingredients:

Boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1.5-inch pieces (3 lbs)
Baby spinach (20 oz)
Yellow onion, finely diced (2 large)
Tomatoes, roma, diced (6)
Garlic cloves, minced (8)
Fresh ginger, grated (2 tbsp)
Nonfat plain yogurt (1/2 cup)
Low-sodium chicken broth (1 cup)
Ground cumin (2 tsp)
Ground coriander (2 tsp)
Turmeric (1 tsp)
Garam masala (2 tsp)
Chili powder (1 tsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Lemon, juiced (1)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/4 cup)

Directions:

1. In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat 1/4 cup broth over medium-high heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring often, for 8 to 10 minutes until softened and starting to brown. Add splashes of broth as needed to prevent sticking.
2. Add the garlic and ginger. Cook for 2 minutes until very fragrant.
3. Add the cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder. Stir constantly for 1 minute to toast the spices.
4. Add the diced tomatoes and 1/2 cup broth. Scrape up any bits from the bottom. Simmer for 8 minutes until the tomatoes break down into a thick sauce.
5. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer. Stir to coat in the sauce. Cook over medium heat for 5 minutes, then add remaining broth, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes.
6. Uncover and stir in all the baby spinach in large handfuls — it will wilt dramatically. Once fully wilted and bright green (about 3 minutes), use an immersion blender to partially blend the spinach sauce directly in the pot, leaving some texture. Alternatively, remove 2 cups of the spinach-sauce mixture, blend it smooth, and stir it back in.
7. Stir in the nonfat yogurt over low heat. Do not boil after adding yogurt or it will curdle. Simmer gently for 5 minutes.
8. Verify chicken is cooked through — internal temperature 165°F. Squeeze lemon juice over the pot, top with fresh cilantro, and serve over brown rice with nonfat yogurt raita on the side.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1.5 cups (chicken + sauce)
Calories: 230 kcal
Total Fat: 4.5 g
Saturated Fat: 1.0 g
Cholesterol: 85 mg
Sodium: 280 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 12 g
Dietary Fiber: 3 g
Sugars: 5.0 g
Protein: 35 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: No added oil — onions are cooked in broth. Nonfat yogurt replaces cream. Spinach adds iron and fiber with zero fat.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Stir 1–2 tsp extra chili powder or a spoonful of chili paste into his bowl. Add fresh sliced serrano or Thai chilies on top. A drizzle of green chutney with extra chili is excellent here.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Very high protein and low calorie per serving. A second serving is fine for both Chris and his wife — this is a weight-loss-friendly meal.

Leftover ideas: Excellent over brown rice, stuffed into a whole-wheat wrap with raw onion and cilantro, or thinned with broth for a quick soup lunch.

Make-ahead / storage: Refrigerate up to 4 days. Freezes well up to 3 months — flavors deepen on day 2.
