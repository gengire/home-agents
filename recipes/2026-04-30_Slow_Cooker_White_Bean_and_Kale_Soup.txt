Title: Slow-Cooker White Bean and Kale Soup
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 6-8 hours on Low (or 3-4 hours on High)
Total Time: 6 hours 15 minutes (Low) or 3 hours 15 minutes (High)

Ingredients:

Cannellini beans, canned, drained and rinsed (2 cans, 15 oz each)
Kale, stems removed, roughly chopped (1 bunch)
Diced tomatoes, canned, no-salt-added (1 can, 14.5 oz)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (4 cups)
Water (1 cup)
Garlic cloves, minced (4)
Yellow onion, diced (1 medium)
Celery stalks, sliced (2)
Carrots, sliced into coins (2 medium)
Dried rosemary (1 tsp)
Dried thyme (1 tsp)
Dried oregano (1 tsp)
Black pepper (to taste)
Red pepper flakes (pinch, optional)
Lemon, juiced (1, added at end)
Whole-grain bread (for serving)

Directions:

1. Add the drained beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, water, garlic, onion, celery, carrots, rosemary, thyme, oregano, black pepper, and red pepper flakes to the slow cooker. Do not add the kale yet.
2. Place the lid on the slow cooker. Cook on Low for 6 to 8 hours or on High for 3 to 4 hours. Avoid lifting the lid during the first half of cooking, as each opening adds 20 to 30 minutes to the cook time.
3. About 30 minutes before serving, stir in the chopped kale. Replace the lid and continue cooking until the kale is fully wilted and tender.
4. Squeeze the lemon juice into the soup, stir to combine, and taste for seasoning. Serve hot with sliced whole-grain bread.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 310 kcal
Total Fat: 1.5 g
Saturated Fat: 0.2 g
Cholesterol: 0 mg
Sodium: 480 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 58 g
Dietary Fiber: 15 g
Protein: 16 g
Additional notes: Cholesterol-free, very high-fiber, excellent source of calcium and iron (kale), and plant-based protein (cannellini beans); add one slice whole-grain bread per serving for an additional ~80 kcal and 2 g fiber

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: A completely plant-based meal and one of the highest-fiber options in the Pritikin program. Cannellini beans and kale are both top-tier "Go" foods. Zero added oil. Rosemary and lemon juice provide bright, complex flavor.
Teen appeal: Hearty and filling — this is a soup that actually satisfies. The crusty whole-grain bread for dipping makes it feel like comfort food. The flavor deepens beautifully over long cooking.
Make-ahead tips: This soup improves overnight as flavors meld. Make a double batch — it freezes well for up to 3 months in airtight containers. Reheat on the stovetop over medium heat with a splash of broth if needed. Safety note: ensure the slow cooker reaches a full simmer during cooking; on a properly functioning slow cooker on Low, this occurs within 2 hours.
