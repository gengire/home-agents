Title: Chana Masala
Category: IND-L — Indian Legume/Dal
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 65 minutes

Ingredients:

Canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed (4 × 15 oz cans)
Yellow onion, finely diced (3 large)
Garlic cloves, minced (10)
Fresh ginger, grated (3 tbsp)
Jalapeño, finely minced (2)
Tomatoes, crushed, canned (2 × 28 oz cans)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (2 cups)
Ground cumin (2 tbsp)
Ground coriander (2 tbsp)
Turmeric (1 tsp)
Garam masala (2 tsp)
Chili powder (1.5 tsp)
Cumin seeds (1 tsp)
Mustard seeds (1 tsp)
Amchur, dry mango powder (1 tsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Lemon, juiced (2)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/2 cup)
Brown rice, cooked, for serving

Directions:

1. In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat 1/4 cup vegetable broth over medium-high heat. Add the cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them toast for 30 to 45 seconds, stirring, until fragrant and the mustard seeds begin to pop.
2. Add the diced onions. Cook for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring often, until deep golden and starting to caramelize. Add splashes of broth a tablespoon at a time to prevent sticking — the goal is golden, not burnt.
3. Add the garlic, ginger, and jalapeño. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the raw smell fades and the mixture is very fragrant.
4. Add the ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric, garam masala, chili powder, and amchur. Stir constantly for 1 full minute to toast the spices directly into the onion base. The pot will be intensely fragrant.
5. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and remaining broth. Stir well, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Simmer over medium heat for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and darkens slightly — this is the masala cooking out.
6. Add all the chickpeas. Stir to coat completely. Use the back of a wooden spoon to mash about one quarter of the chickpeas against the side of the pot — this creates a thick, creamy sauce while keeping most chickpeas whole and intact.
7. Reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer uncovered for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is very thick and coats the chickpeas heavily.
8. Stir in the lemon juice, black pepper, and most of the cilantro. Taste and adjust salt or lemon. Serve over brown rice, topped with remaining cilantro and nonfat yogurt on the side.

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Entirely plant-based, zero added oil — onions and spices cooked in broth. Chickpeas are among the best Pritikin "Go" foods: very high fiber, very high protein, negligible fat. Amchur adds tartness and depth without any fat or added sugar.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Stir 1 tsp chili paste or a full sliced serrano into his bowl. A sprig of green chutney with extra chili over the top is excellent. Add 1 tsp extra chili powder directly into his serving and finish with fresh sliced Thai chilies.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Very high fiber and protein, low calorie density. A second serving is absolutely fine for both Chris and his wife — this is one of the most weight-loss-friendly meals in the rotation. The high fiber supports satiety for hours.

Leftover ideas: Chana masala is genuinely better on day 2 — flavors deepen significantly overnight. Stuff into a toasted whole-wheat pita with raw onion and cucumber. Thin with a splash of broth and use as a grain bowl sauce over barley or quinoa.

Make-ahead / storage: Refrigerate up to 5 days. Freezes exceptionally well for up to 3 months. Double the batch and freeze half for a future week with zero additional effort.
