Title: Mango Lassi Parfait
Category: DESS — Dessert
Servings: 4
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 0 minutes
Total Time: 10 minutes

Ingredients:

Nonfat plain yogurt (2 cups)
Mango, fresh or frozen, diced (2 cups)
Cardamom, ground (1/2 tsp)
Lemon, juiced (1)
Rolled oats, toasted dry (1/4 cup)
Fresh mint leaves (small handful, optional)

Directions:

1. If using frozen mango, thaw at room temperature for 15 minutes or microwave for 60 seconds. Pat dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
2. Toast the rolled oats in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring constantly, until golden and fragrant. Remove from heat immediately and let cool.
3. Stir the cardamom and lemon juice into the nonfat yogurt. Taste — the yogurt should have a light, fragrant sweetness from the cardamom.
4. Build parfaits in 4 glasses or bowls: start with a layer of yogurt, then a layer of mango, then yogurt again, topped with mango.
5. Sprinkle toasted oats over the top of each parfait. Garnish with fresh mint leaves if using. Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to 2 hours before serving.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1 parfait (approx. 3/4 cup yogurt + 1/2 cup mango)
Calories: 175 kcal
Total Fat: 1.0 g
Saturated Fat: 0.2 g
Cholesterol: 5 mg
Sodium: 85 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 34 g
Dietary Fiber: 3 g
Sugars: 26.0 g
Protein: 10 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Nonfat yogurt, whole fruit, and dry-toasted oats — no added sugar, no butter, no cream. Mango provides natural sweetness; cardamom is one of the best ways to make a dessert taste rich and complex without any fat or sugar.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Not applicable — this is a cooling dessert. Chris may enjoy a pinch of cayenne or black pepper on his mango for a spice-sweet contrast if he wants heat.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: A light, high-protein dessert. Appropriate for both adults as a single serving. The yogurt protein and fiber help prevent post-dessert snacking.

Leftover ideas: No real leftovers — assemble fresh. Pre-portion the yogurt and mango separately in the fridge and assemble each serving as needed rather than building all four at once if you want them to last.

Make-ahead / storage: Toasted oats keep in an airtight container for 2 weeks. Yogurt and mango layers can be pre-portioned and stored separately for up to 3 days — assemble just before serving to keep the oat topping crisp.
