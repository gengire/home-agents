Title: Black Bean and Sweet Potato Enchilada Casserole
Category: MEX — Mexican/Latin
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 65 minutes

Ingredients:

Black beans, canned, drained and rinsed (2 cans, 15 oz each)
Sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed into 3/4-inch pieces (3 large)
Yellow onion, finely diced (1 large)
Garlic cloves, minced (4)
Jalapeño, finely minced (2)
Diced tomatoes, canned, no-salt-added (1 can, 14.5 oz)
Enchilada sauce, no-oil (2 cups)
Whole-grain corn tortillas (12)
Nonfat plain yogurt (1/2 cup)
Ground cumin (2 tsp)
Chili powder (2 tsp)
Smoked paprika (1 tsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (1/4 cup)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/3 cup)
Lime, juiced (2)

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Steam or microwave sweet potato cubes until just fork-tender but not mushy, about 8 minutes in the microwave or 10 minutes steamed. Set aside.
3. In a large skillet, heat 2 tbsp broth over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook for 6 to 7 minutes until softened. Add garlic and jalapeño, cook 2 more minutes.
4. Add the cumin, chili powder, and smoked paprika. Stir for 1 minute to toast.
5. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes, lime juice, and black pepper. Stir and cook for 5 minutes until the mixture thickens slightly. Fold in the cooked sweet potato cubes gently — you want them to hold their shape.
6. Spread 1/2 cup enchilada sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
7. Layer half the corn tortillas over the sauce, overlapping as needed to cover the bottom.
8. Spread half the bean and sweet potato filling over the tortillas. Pour 3/4 cup enchilada sauce over the filling.
9. Add another layer of tortillas, the remaining filling, and pour the rest of the enchilada sauce over the top.
10. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake uncovered for another 10 minutes until the edges are bubbling and the top is slightly crisp.
11. Let rest 5 minutes before serving. Top with nonfat yogurt (in place of sour cream) and fresh cilantro.

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Entirely plant-based, no added oil, very high fiber. Sweet potatoes and black beans are both top-tier Pritikin "Go" foods. Check enchilada sauce label — choose a brand with no added oil and under 300mg sodium per 1/4 cup.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Add extra minced jalapeño to his portion during plating, or stir a tsp of chipotle chili powder into just his section of the casserole before the second bake. Serve his plate with fresh sliced serrano on the side and a spoonful of salsa verde.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Calorie-dense from the sweet potatoes and tortillas — one generous serving is recommended for weight loss. Add a large side salad with lime-vinegar dressing or steamed broccoli to fill the plate without adding significant calories.

Leftover ideas: Excellent cold or room temperature for lunch. Reheat individual portions in a 350°F oven for 10 minutes rather than microwaving — it keeps the tortilla layers from going soggy.

Make-ahead / storage: Assemble up to 24 hours ahead and refrigerate before baking — this actually improves the flavor. Add 10 minutes to bake time if going straight from cold. Refrigerate leftovers up to 4 days.
