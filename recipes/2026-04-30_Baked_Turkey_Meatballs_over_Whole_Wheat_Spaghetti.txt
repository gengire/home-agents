Title: Baked Turkey Meatballs over Whole-Wheat Spaghetti with Marinara
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes

Ingredients:

Lean ground turkey (1 lb)
Oat flour (1/2 cup, or blend 1/2 cup rolled oats until fine)
Egg white (1 large)
Garlic cloves, minced (3)
Fresh parsley, chopped (2 tbsp)
Dried oregano (1 tsp)
Fennel seed (1/2 tsp, optional)
Black pepper (1/4 tsp)
Nonfat milk or unsweetened soymilk (2 tbsp)
Whole-wheat spaghetti (12 oz)
Marinara sauce, no-sugar-added (1 jar, 24 oz)
Fresh basil leaves (for garnish, optional)

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. If making your own oat flour, add 1/2 cup rolled oats to a blender and blend until a fine powder forms, about 30 seconds.
3. Combine the ground turkey, oat flour, egg white, minced garlic, parsley, oregano, fennel seed, black pepper, and milk in a large bowl. Mix gently with your hands or a fork until just combined — do not overwork the mixture or the meatballs will become dense and tough.
4. Roll the mixture into balls approximately 1.5 inches in diameter, roughly the size of a golf ball. You should get 18 to 20 meatballs. Place them on the parchment-lined baking sheet with space between each one so they bake rather than steam.
5. Bake for 22 to 25 minutes until the meatballs are firm, lightly browned on the outside, and reach an internal temperature of 165°F when tested with a meat thermometer inserted into the center of the largest meatball.
6. While the meatballs bake, cook the whole-wheat spaghetti in a large pot of boiling water according to package directions. Simultaneously, warm the marinara in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally.
7. Drain the cooked pasta without rinsing. Plate the spaghetti, ladle marinara over the top, and add meatballs. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 665 kcal
Total Fat: 15.5 g
Saturated Fat: 3.5 g
Cholesterol: 90 mg
Sodium: 500 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 87 g
Dietary Fiber: 12 g
Protein: 39 g
Additional notes: High-fiber whole-wheat pasta and oat flour make this significantly more nutritious than traditional spaghetti and meatballs; good source of iron and B vitamins

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Oat flour replaces traditional breadcrumbs, providing whole-grain fiber without refined carbohydrates. The egg white binds without the fat of a whole egg. No added oil — the meatballs bake on parchment and release enough moisture from the turkey to stay moist. Choose a marinara with no added sugar and less than 400mg sodium per serving.
Teen appeal: Spaghetti and meatballs is universally loved; this version tastes indistinguishable from traditional meatballs. The fennel seed gives an authentic Italian sausage flavor without any processed meat. Let teens pile on extra marinara.
Make-ahead tips: Meatballs freeze beautifully. Make a double batch, bake all of them, cool completely, and freeze in a zip-lock bag for up to 3 months. Reheat directly in simmering marinara for 10 minutes from frozen. Safety note: use a meat thermometer to verify the center of the largest meatball reaches 165°F — the outside browns well before the inside is fully cooked.
