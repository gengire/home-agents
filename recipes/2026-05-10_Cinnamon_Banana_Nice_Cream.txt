Title: Cinnamon Banana Nice Cream
Category: DESS — Dessert
Servings: 4–6 (one generous dessert for the family)
Prep Time: 5 minutes active (plus at least 8 hours freezing time — freeze bananas by Wednesday evening for Friday)
Cook Time: 0 minutes
Total Time: 5 minutes active

Ingredients:

Ripe bananas, peeled, sliced into 1-inch rounds, frozen solid (6 large)
Cinnamon, ground (2 tsp)
Vanilla extract (1 tsp)
Nonfat plain yogurt (1/4 cup)

Optional add-ins (choose one):
Unsweetened cocoa powder (2 tbsp) for a chocolate version
Fresh berries for topping
Almond extract (1/4 tsp) instead of vanilla

Directions:

1. Prepare ahead: Peel the bananas, slice into 1-inch rounds, and spread in a single layer on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Freeze for at least 8 hours or overnight until completely solid. Transfer to a zip bag for storage. (Do this by Wednesday evening for Friday dessert — they keep in the freezer up to 3 months.)
2. When ready to serve, let the frozen banana slices sit at room temperature for 3 to 5 minutes to take the hard edge off — this helps the food processor run smoothly without straining the motor.
3. Add the frozen banana slices to a food processor. Pulse 10 times to break them down, then process continuously for 2 to 3 minutes, scraping down the sides once or twice. The banana will go through stages: first crumbly, then chunky and grainy, then suddenly smooth and creamy like soft-serve ice cream.
4. Add the ground cinnamon, vanilla extract, and nonfat yogurt. Process for another 30 seconds until fully combined and silky smooth.
5. Taste and adjust — add a touch more cinnamon if desired.
6. Serve immediately in bowls for a soft-serve consistency. For a firmer, scoopable texture, transfer to a freezer-safe container and freeze for 1 hour before serving.
7. Top with optional fresh berries or an extra pinch of cinnamon.

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: The entire dessert is essentially frozen banana — zero added fat, zero refined sugar. The sweetness comes entirely from ripe bananas. The nonfat yogurt adds creaminess without adding fat. This is one of the most Pritikin-compliant desserts possible: whole fruit, no fat, no added sugar.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: N/A — cooling dessert. For a contrast pairing, Chris can sprinkle Tajín chili-lime seasoning on top of his bowl — the chili-lime plays unexpectedly well against sweet banana.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Moderate in natural sugars from the bananas — one serving is appropriate on a weight-loss plan. Because the sugars are from whole fruit, they come packaged with fiber and potassium. A second serving is fine for the daughters; Chris and his wife should stick to one serving given weight loss goals.

Leftover ideas: Store finished nice cream in a sealed freezer container for up to 1 week. Let thaw for 5 minutes before scooping. Keep a bag of pre-sliced frozen bananas in the freezer perpetually — it becomes a pantry staple for instant desserts any night.

Make-ahead / storage: Frozen banana slices keep for up to 3 months in a zip bag. Finished nice cream keeps up to 1 week frozen. Do not refrigerate — it will turn to mush.
