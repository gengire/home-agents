Title: Lemon-Herb Baked Tilapia with Roasted Vegetables and Quinoa
Category: FISH — Fish/Seafood
Servings: 10–12 (feeds 4 for dinner twice, with room for seconds)
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 35 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes

Ingredients:

Tilapia fillets (4 lbs)
Quinoa, dry (2 cups)
Low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth (4 cups)
Bell peppers, cut into 1-inch pieces (4, mixed colors)
Zucchini, cut into half-moons (3 medium)
Cherry tomatoes (1 pint)
Garlic cloves, minced (6)
Lemon, zested and juiced (3)
Fresh dill, chopped (3 tbsp)
Fresh parsley, chopped (3 tbsp)
Dried oregano (1.5 tsp)
Black pepper (1 tsp)
Low-sodium vegetable broth for roasting (1/4 cup)
Balsamic vinegar (2 tbsp)

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line two sheet pans with parchment paper.
2. Cook quinoa: rinse thoroughly in a fine-mesh strainer. Combine with 4 cups broth in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, reduce to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat, let steam 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork. Stir in 1/3 of the minced garlic, half the parsley, and the zest of 1 lemon.
3. Toss the bell peppers and zucchini with 2 tbsp broth, balsamic vinegar, oregano, half the remaining garlic, and half the black pepper. Spread on one sheet pan. Roast for 20 minutes.
4. After 20 minutes, push the vegetables to one side and add the cherry tomatoes to the pan. Toss to coat with the pan juices.
5. Place tilapia fillets on the second sheet pan. Season with remaining garlic, dill, remaining black pepper, and squeeze lemon juice generously over each fillet.
6. Place tilapia in the oven and roast both pans for 10 to 12 minutes until the tilapia flakes easily with a fork and reaches an internal temperature of 145°F. The flesh should be fully opaque — no translucent center.
7. Serve tilapia over quinoa with roasted vegetables alongside. Squeeze remaining lemon juice over everything and garnish with remaining parsley.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1 fillet + 1 cup quinoa + 1 cup roasted vegetables
Calories: 310 kcal
Total Fat: 3.0 g
Saturated Fat: 0.5 g
Cholesterol: 65 mg
Sodium: 230 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 38 g
Dietary Fiber: 6 g
Sugars: 7.0 g
Protein: 32 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Tilapia is one of the leanest fish proteins available — very low fat with a mild flavor that takes on herbs and citrus beautifully. No oil used — broth and balsamic vinegar serve as the roasting liquid. Quinoa is a complete protein whole grain.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Sprinkle red pepper flakes generously over his cod before baking, or add fresh sliced jalapeño to his plate. A spoonful of harissa (check label for oil — choose oil-free) works well with the lemon-herb profile.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Very low calorie and fat for a complete meal. A second serving is ideal for Chris given his size and satiety needs. His wife can comfortably have a second serving too.

Leftover ideas: Cold tilapia over mixed greens with lemon vinegar dressing makes an excellent no-reheat lunch. Flake leftover tilapia into the quinoa with a squeeze of lemon for a quick grain salad.

Make-ahead / storage: Quinoa and roasted vegetables keep up to 4 days refrigerated. Tilapia is best fresh — if reheating, use a 300°F oven for 8 minutes rather than microwave to avoid rubbery texture.
