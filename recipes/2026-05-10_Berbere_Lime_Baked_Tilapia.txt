Title: Berbere-Lime Baked Tilapia
Category: FISH — Fish/Seafood
Servings: 8 (feeds 4 for dinner with room for seconds — single-night preparation only; fish does not reheat well)
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

Tilapia fillets, thawed (8 medium fillets, about 3 lbs total)
Berbere spice blend (2 tbsp)
Lime, juiced and zested (2)
Garlic cloves, minced (4)
Fresh ginger, grated (1 tbsp)
Low-sodium vegetable broth (3 tbsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Fresh cilantro, chopped (1/4 cup)
Lime wedges for serving

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a small bowl, combine the berbere spice blend, lime zest, lime juice, minced garlic, grated ginger, and vegetable broth. Stir to form a thick, spreadable paste.
3. Pat the tilapia fillets thoroughly dry with paper towels — this is critical for achieving a spice crust rather than steaming under the paste.
4. Lay the fillets in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet. Spoon and spread the berbere paste evenly over the top of each fillet, using about 1.5 tsp paste per piece.
5. Bake at 425°F for 16 to 20 minutes until the fish flakes easily with a fork — the internal temperature should reach 145°F and the spice crust will be set and darkened.
6. Squeeze a fresh lime wedge over the entire tray immediately out of the oven. Top with fresh cilantro. Serve immediately alongside Kik Alicha.

Berbere Spice Blend (DIY if not purchased): Combine 1 tbsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp cayenne + 1/2 tsp ground ginger + 1/2 tsp ground coriander + 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon + 1/4 tsp ground allspice + 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg + 1/2 tsp garlic powder + 1/2 tsp black pepper. Makes about 2.5 tbsp — perfect for this recipe with a small amount to spare.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1 fillet (about 6 oz) with spice crust
Calories: 170 kcal
Total Fat: 3.5 g
Saturated Fat: 0.8 g
Cholesterol: 85 mg
Sodium: 175 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 3 g
Dietary Fiber: 0.5 g
Sugars: 0.5 g
Protein: 32 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Zero added oil — the berbere paste uses only spice, lime, and a splash of broth. Tilapia is one of the leanest white fish proteins available. Baking at high heat produces a satisfying spice crust without any fat.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Double the cayenne in the berbere paste on 2 extra fillets set aside for him. Add fresh sliced serrano or Thai chilies directly on top of his fillets before baking. Serve his plate with a side of harissa or chili paste for dipping.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Tilapia is one of the leanest proteins per calorie — extraordinarily low calorie per gram of protein. A second fillet is absolutely fine and actively encouraged for both adults working toward weight loss. Pair with the high-fiber Kik Alicha for a supremely filling Pritikin dinner.

Leftover ideas: Fish does not reheat well — plan this as a single-night meal only. The spice crust becomes soggy when refrigerated. The Kik Alicha served alongside will carry forward to Wednesday as the leftover meal. Any excess cooked tilapia could be flaked cold into a salad the next day, but do not reheat.

Make-ahead / storage: Prepare the berbere paste up to 2 days ahead and refrigerate. Thaw tilapia in the refrigerator the night before. Do not freeze or microwave cooked tilapia.
