Title: Turkey and Vegetable Stir-Fry over Brown Rice
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients:

Lean ground turkey (1 lb)
Brown rice, cooked (3 cups)
Snap peas (2 cups)
Bell peppers, thinly sliced (2, mixed colors)
Mushrooms, sliced (8 oz)
Bok choy, stems and leaves separated, chopped (1 small head)
Garlic cloves, minced (4)
Fresh ginger, grated (1 tbsp)
Low-sodium soy sauce or tamari (3 tbsp)
Rice vinegar (1 tbsp)
Red pepper flakes (to taste)
Sesame seeds (1 tsp, optional)
Low-sodium chicken broth (2 tbsp)

Directions:

1. Warm cooked brown rice in a covered saucepan over low heat with a splash of water, or microwave covered until heated through. Set aside.
2. Heat a large nonstick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the 2 tbsp of broth or water in place of oil.
3. Add the ground turkey and break it apart with a wooden spoon. Cook for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring frequently, until no pink remains throughout and the internal temperature reads 165°F. Do not rush this step — the turkey must be fully cooked.
4. Push cooked turkey to the side of the pan. Add the garlic and ginger to the cleared space and cook for 30 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
5. Add the sliced mushrooms and bok choy stems to the pan. Stir-fry for 3 to 4 minutes, tossing everything together with the turkey.
6. Add the bell peppers and snap peas. Stir-fry for another 3 minutes — the vegetables should be bright in color and slightly crisp, not limp.
7. Add the bok choy leaves, soy sauce, and rice vinegar. Toss everything together and cook for 1 to 2 more minutes until the leaves are just wilted.
8. Serve the stir-fry over warm brown rice. Garnish with red pepper flakes and sesame seeds if desired.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 465 kcal
Total Fat: 12.0 g
Saturated Fat: 3.0 g
Cholesterol: 90 mg
Sodium: 580 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 49 g
Dietary Fiber: 6 g
Protein: 33 g
Additional notes: High protein, moderate sodium (primarily from low-sodium soy sauce); rich in vitamins C and K from bell peppers and bok choy

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Lean ground turkey with no added oil — broth or water is used for the cooking liquid. All vegetables are Pritikin "Go" foods. Soy sauce provides deep umami flavor without any fat.
Teen appeal: Stir-fry has bold, savory flavors and colorful vegetables that look appealing on the plate. Teens can control their own heat level with the red pepper flakes. Familiar takeout-style flavor at home.
Make-ahead tips: Slice all vegetables up to 2 days ahead and store in containers in the fridge. Brown rice cooked earlier in the week works perfectly here — this is a great mid-week use of Sunday meal prep. Safety note: ground turkey must reach 165°F internally; cut into the thickest portion to confirm there is no pink.
