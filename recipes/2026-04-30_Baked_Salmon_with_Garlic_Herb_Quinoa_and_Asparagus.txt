Title: Baked Salmon with Garlic-Herb Quinoa and Steamed Asparagus
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients:

Salmon fillets, 5-6 oz each (4)
Quinoa, dry (1 1/2 cups)
Low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth (3 cups)
Asparagus, tough ends snapped off (1 bunch)
Garlic cloves, minced (4)
Lemon, zested and juiced (1)
Fresh dill, chopped (2 tbsp)
Dried dill (1 tsp, if substituting for fresh)
Fresh parsley, chopped (1 tbsp)
Black pepper (to taste)
Tomatoes, sliced (2 medium, for serving)
Balsamic vinegar (for serving)

Directions:

1. Rinse the quinoa thoroughly in a fine mesh strainer under cold running water to remove bitterness. Combine rinsed quinoa and broth in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let steam, still covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and stir in half the minced garlic, the parsley, and the lemon zest.
2. Preheat oven to 400°F. Place salmon fillets on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Season each fillet with black pepper, the chopped dill, remaining garlic, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
3. Bake salmon for 12 to 15 minutes until it flakes easily when pressed gently with a fork and the internal temperature reads 145°F on a meat thermometer. The flesh should be fully opaque throughout — no translucent or raw-looking center.
4. While salmon bakes, steam asparagus in a steamer basket over 1 inch of boiling water for 4 to 5 minutes, until bright green and just tender when pierced with a knife. Alternatively, roast asparagus on a second sheet pan at 400°F for 10 to 12 minutes alongside the salmon.
5. Serve salmon over a bed of quinoa with asparagus on the side and sliced tomatoes drizzled with balsamic vinegar.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 555 kcal
Total Fat: 17.5 g
Saturated Fat: 3.5 g
Cholesterol: 85 mg
Sodium: 260 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 43 g
Dietary Fiber: 7 g
Protein: 53 g
Additional notes: Excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids (salmon), complete protein (quinoa), and folate (asparagus); low in saturated fat

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Omega-3-rich salmon is a Pritikin-approved "Go" protein. Quinoa is a complete whole-grain protein. No added oil — lemon juice and broth provide all the moisture needed for cooking.
Teen appeal: Salmon with garlic and herbs has approachable flavors; the bright lemon aroma makes it feel restaurant-quality. The quinoa base absorbs the pan juices beautifully.
Make-ahead tips: Quinoa can be made up to 3 days ahead and refrigerated. Reheat with a splash of broth. Salmon is best cooked fresh — it becomes rubbery when reheated. Safety note: 145°F yields moist, safe salmon; going to 150°F is fine for those who prefer well-done, but avoid going higher.
