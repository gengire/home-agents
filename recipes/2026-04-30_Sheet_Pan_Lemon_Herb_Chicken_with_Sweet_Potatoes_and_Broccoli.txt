Title: Sheet Pan Lemon-Herb Chicken with Sweet Potatoes and Broccoli
Servings: 4 generous
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 35 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

Boneless, skinless chicken thighs (2 lbs)
Sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed into 1-inch pieces (2 large)
Broccoli, cut into florets (1 large head)
Garlic cloves, minced (4)
Lemon, zested and juiced (1)
Dried oregano (1 tsp)
Dried thyme (1 tsp)
Black pepper (1/2 tsp)
Low-sodium chicken broth (1/4 cup)
Red pepper flakes (pinch)

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 425°F and line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Toss sweet potato cubes with half the minced garlic, a splash of chicken broth, the oregano, and black pepper. Spread on one side of the pan.
3. Roast the sweet potatoes alone for 15 minutes while you prepare the chicken.
4. Pat chicken thighs dry with paper towels. Rub with remaining garlic, lemon zest, thyme, black pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Place on the pan alongside the sweet potatoes.
5. Add broccoli florets to any remaining space on the pan. Pour the rest of the broth over everything.
6. Roast for an additional 20 to 22 minutes (35 to 37 minutes total) until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F on a meat thermometer, the sweet potatoes are fork-tender, and the broccoli edges are lightly charred.
7. Squeeze the remaining lemon juice over everything before serving.

Nutrition (per serving):

Calories: 460 kcal
Total Fat: 12.5 g
Saturated Fat: 3.5 g
Cholesterol: 115 mg
Sodium: 240 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 50 g
Dietary Fiber: 10 g
Protein: 41 g
Additional notes: Low-sodium, high-fiber, excellent source of vitamin A (sweet potatoes), vitamin C (broccoli), and lean protein

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: Lean skinless poultry roasted without added oil using broth to prevent sticking. All components are Pritikin "Go" foods — lean protein, starchy vegetable, and cruciferous vegetable.
Teen appeal: Crispy-edged broccoli and caramelized sweet potatoes are crowd-pleasers; the lemon-herb aroma makes this smell amazing coming out of the oven.
Make-ahead tips: Cube the sweet potatoes and cut broccoli up to 2 days ahead and store covered in the fridge. Leftovers reheat well in a 375°F oven for 10 minutes. Internal temp safety note: chicken thighs can appear slightly pink near the bone even when fully cooked — always verify with a thermometer reading of 165°F.
