Title: Berry Oat Parfait
Category: DESS — Dessert
Servings: 4
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 5 minutes
Total Time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

Nonfat plain yogurt (2 cups)
Mixed berries, fresh or frozen, thawed (2 cups)
Rolled oats, toasted dry (1/2 cup)
Lemon, zested and juiced (1)
Cinnamon, ground (1/2 tsp)
Vanilla extract, pure (1/2 tsp)

Directions:

1. Toast the rolled oats in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring constantly, until golden and fragrant. Remove from heat immediately and spread on a plate to cool. They will crisp up as they cool.
2. If using frozen berries, thaw at room temperature or microwave for 60 seconds. Drain any excess liquid.
3. Stir the lemon zest, lemon juice, cinnamon, and vanilla extract into the nonfat yogurt until well combined.
4. Build each parfait in a glass or bowl: a layer of yogurt, then a generous layer of berries, then yogurt again, topped with berries.
5. Spoon toasted oats over each parfait just before serving to keep them crispy.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Serving Size: 1 parfait (approx. 3/4 cup yogurt + 1/2 cup berries)
Calories: 160 kcal
Total Fat: 1.5 g
Saturated Fat: 0.2 g
Cholesterol: 5 mg
Sodium: 75 mg
Total Carbohydrate: 28 g
Dietary Fiber: 4 g
Sugars: 14.0 g
Protein: 10 g

Notes:

Pritikin compliance: No added sugar — vanilla and cinnamon make the yogurt taste lightly sweet and complex without any sweetener. Berries are among the top Pritikin-approved fruits due to their exceptionally high fiber and antioxidant content relative to their sugar load.

🌶️ Chris's Heat Upgrade: Not applicable for dessert. Chris can add a crack of black pepper over the berries if he wants a spice note — pepper with strawberries and blackberries is actually a classic flavor pairing.

⚖️ Weight Loss Note: Light and protein-rich. One serving is a satisfying dessert for both adults. The yogurt protein and berry fiber together help prevent late-night snacking.

Leftover ideas: Store yogurt mixture and berries separately in the fridge and assemble fresh per serving. Toasted oats should be stored dry at room temperature.

Make-ahead / storage: Toasted oats keep in an airtight container for 2 weeks. Pre-portioned yogurt keeps 3–4 days. Assembling ahead makes the oats soggy — always top just before serving.
